# **0009: Objective Mirror Philosophy for Insights**

**Context:** When a specific match has an extreme pace (e.g., a highly defensive, slow 3v3 game), multiple mechanical metrics like BPM, Average Speed, and Shots will naturally drop significantly below a player's Historical Baseline. Previously, there was concern that highlighting these drops would feel like the app was "yelling at" the player for making tactically sound, defensive choices.  
**Decision:** The Insight Engine will act as an "Objective Mirror" rather than an "Opinionated Coach." It will not attempt to artificially normalize stats based on the subjective "pace" or "flow" of a game. If a player's BPM is 30% lower than their baseline, the Engine will objectively report it as an outlier. It is strictly the player's responsibility to contextualize that data tactically (e.g., "Yes, my BPM is low because I am playing third-man defense.").  
**Consequences:** The Engine's mathematical derivation remains clean, deterministic, and free of highly subjective "game pace" modifier variables. The user interface must rely on neutral, objective phrasing (e.g., "BPM is 30% lower than normal") rather than judgmental phrasing (e.g., "You are playing too slow").
